Note that [Morpheme kontomopl](MorphsetTable?#cldf:kontomopl) appears to usually be restricted to verbs, while *-santomo* is found with third person pronouns and demonstratives.

## Nominal inflection {#sec:nouninfl}

Nouns in Yawarana may bear suffixes marking their possession status (<a href='#sec:nounposssuf' class='crossref' name='sec:nounposssuf' release="False" >ref</a>), number (<a href='#sec:nominalnumber' class='crossref' name='sec:nominalnumber' release="False" >ref</a>), and nominal past tense (<a href='#sec:nominaltense' class='crossref' name='sec:nominaltense' release="False" >ref</a>).
Possessed nouns may bear a person prefix, or the linker [Morpheme ylk](MorphsetTable?no_translation#cldf:ylk) (<a href='#sec:nominalperson' class='crossref' name='sec:nominalperson' release="False" >ref</a>). 

<span title='noun classes re: possession'>❗️</span>


### Suffixes for possessed and non-possessed nouns {#sec:nounposssuf}

In the possession construction in Yawarana, the possessor noun occurs immediately preceding the possessed noun, which is the head of the possession phrase. <span title='crossref to phrase structure'>❗️</span>
Alternatively, the possessor can appear as a prefix on the possessed noun.
The possessor noun is never marked (for instance, with genitive case), but the possessed noun (the head) is often marked for being possessed by a suffix; an unambiguous label for this counterpart of the genitive is pertensive ([dixon2010basic](sources.bib?with_internal_ref_link&ref#cldf:dixon2010basic)).
The choice of suffix is lexically conditioned; while most nouns take [Morpheme rupert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:rupert), some take [Morpheme tipert](MorphsetTable?no_translation#cldf:tipert).
Unpossessed nouns generally are unmarked, but some 15 nouns <span title='which? list nouns'>❓</span> bear the suffix [Morpheme tenpert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:tenpert) when they appear without a possessor. 

Examples <a class="exref" example_id="onlypossessed"release="False"  end="unsuffixednouns" ></a> illustrate the possible patterns of markedness for nouns when possessed and unpossessed.
The vast majority of nouns in our corpus are unmarked when unpossessed, but when possessed the suffix [Morpheme rupert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:rupert) occurs <a class="exref" example_id="onlypossessed"release="False" ></a>.
A handful of nouns <span title='i.e., the 15?'>❓</span> is marked with [Morpheme rupert](MorphsetTable?no_translation#cldf:rupert)/[Morpheme tipert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:tipert) when possessed and with [Morpheme tenpert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:tenpert) when not possessed <a class="exref" example_id="diffpossessed"release="False" ></a>.
Another handful is unmarked when possessed and marked with [Morpheme tenpert](MorphsetTable?#cldf:tenpert) when not possessed <a class="exref" example_id="suffunpossessed"release="False" ></a>.
The fourth logical category, where neither possession or non-possession is marked, contains very few members (only one attested so far).
For these nouns, the difference is marked only by the presence or absence of a possessive prefix or free-form possessor <a class="exref" example_id="unsuffixednouns"release="False" ></a>.

### Argument prefixes {#sec:nominalperson}
Person prefixes on nouns are conditioned by the initial segment (<a href='#tab:possprefixes' class='crossref' name='tab:possprefixes' release="False" >ref</a>).
C-initial nouns take [Morph i31](MorphTable?#cldf:i31), and first and second person are bare [Morph u1](MorphTable?#cldf:u1) and [Morph me2](MorphTable?#cldf:me2).
On V-initial nouns, third person is marked with [Morph it3](MorphTable?#cldf:it3), and the first and second person prefixes combine with the linker [Morpheme ylk](MorphsetTable?#cldf:ylk).
Some examples are shown in <a class="exref" example_id="ctorat-23"release="False"  end="lastex" ></a>.
