The pluractional marker -pëtï

This aspect-marking morpheme is known from other Cariban languages Mattiola and Gildea under review. This example illustrates its allomorph:

(ctorat-40)
tipapëjsejne waijtajne
tipa-pëj-se-jne go.group-PLUR-PST-PL
waijta-jne mouse-PL
‘las ratas se fueron’

-jpë

-jpë was originally a nominalizer, but now also functions as a simple past marker:

(anfoperso-02)
ana këyetajpë, intipijkë ana chi yawë
ana 1+3
këyeta-jpë grow.up-PST
intipijkë a.little
ana 1+3
chi-∅ COP-IPFV
yawë LOC
‘nos criamos cuando estábamos chiquiticos nosotros’

It also occurs on nouns:

(anfoperso-17)
tawara ma ana këyetajpë, ana papa pan patajpë të
tawara too
ma-∅ throw-IPFV
ana 1+3
këyeta-jpë grow.up-PST
ana 1+3
papa father:VOC
pan late
pata-jpë axe-PST
të-∅ go-IPFV
‘así nos criamos después de que se murió mi papá en su pueblo’
