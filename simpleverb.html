    13.1 Intransitive clauses

    13.1.1 First person

    1. Preverbal pronoun
      wïrë yaruwarijra
      wïrë wïrë 1pro
      yaruwari =jra yaruwa -ri =jra laugh-ipfv=neg
      ‘I don’t laugh.’ (Conversación sobre la risa entre GrMe y ElPe : 4)
    1. Postverbal pronoun
      këyaja wïrë
      këyaja këya -ja think-neg
      wïrë wïrë 1pro
      ‘no sé (auto)’ (Conversación sobre malos augurios y otros : 298)
    1. Prefix
      uyïwïj yawë usenejkari sukuri jwama
      uyïwïj u- y- ïwïj -∅ 1-lk-house-pert
      yawë yawë loc
      usenejkari u- -ri 1-stay-ipfv
      sukuri sukuri silently
      jwama   ***
      ‘I silently stay in my house.’ (Conversación sobre la risa entre GrMe y ElPe : 28)
    1. Preverbal pronoun and prefix
      aaa usukuru morone ta, wïrë usujta ta ne
      aaa aaa ah
      usukuru u- suku -ru 1-urine-pert
      morone morone hurting
      ta ta like
      wïrë wïrë 1pro
      usujta u- -∅ 1-urinate-ipfv
      ta ta like
      ne ne ints
      ‘My urine hurts, I will urinate.’ (Cuento de las ratas por AnFo : 23)
    • not attested: prefixed verb followed by pronoun

    13.1.2 Second person

    1. Prefix
      tototomo pata yaka mëtëja
      tototomo toto -tomo non_Indian-pl
      pata pata -∅ village-pert
      yaka yaka all
      mëtëja më- të -ja 2-go-neg
      ‘you can't (can't) go to the creoles' village (auto)’ (Conversation about family between AmGu and ElPe : 7)
    • not attested: pronoun

    13.1.3 Third person

    1. Zero
      wïnïjse
      wïnïjse wïnïj -se sleep-pst
      ‘He slept.’ (Cuento de las ratas por AnFo : 6)
    1. Preverbal NP
      irëjpë tëwï waijtatomo nwajtëri
      irëjpë irëjpë then
      tëwï tëwï 3pro
      waijtatomo waijta -tomo mouse-pl
      nwajtëri nwajtë -ri dance-ipfv
      ‘Then the mice were dancing.’ (Cuento de las ratas por AnFo : 16)
    1. Postverbal NP
      wepïrï makë
      wepïrï wepï -rï come-ipfv
      makë makë mom
      ‘mi mamá comes (auto)’ (Conversation about dreams with IrDi and ElPe : 83)
    1. Preverbal pronoun
      tëwï nwajtënëpëkë
      tëwï tëwï 3pro
      nwajtënëpëkë nwajtë -nëpëkë dance-prog.intr
      ‘he... he was dancing all the time. (auto)’ (Descripción de la construcción de una casa e historia de la muerte de Agustín : 28)
    1. Postverbal pronoun
      ë'ë, tawara takï chapëtiri tëwï
      ë'ë ë'ë yes
      tawara tawara too
      takï takï cnfrm
      chapëtiri   ***
      tëwï tëwï 3pro
      ‘yes, it also shouts that (auto)’ (Conversación sobre malos augurios y otros : 113)
    • të has an irregular third person marker ij-
    1. waraijtokon maniki irëjpë ijtëse
      waraijtokon waraijtokon man
      maniki maniki rel.anim
      irëjpë irëjpë then
      ijtëse ij- të -se 3-go-pst
      ‘Then the man went.’ (Cuento de las ratas por AnFo : 45)

    13.2 Transitive clauses

    There are several factors that play a role here:

    1. presence or absence of prefix
    2. presence or absence of pronouns and NPs
    3. order of free arguments
    4. presence or absence of ya

    13.2.1 Third on third

    1. preverbal pronoun with ya
      tëwï ya nepïjpë wejsapë
      tëwï tëwï 3pro
      ya ya erg
      nepïjpë nepï -jpë bring-pst
      wejsapë wej -sapë cop-pfv
      ‘he brought it (auto)’ (Conversación cosas no indígenas : 132)
