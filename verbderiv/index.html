    5. Verbal roots and stems

    5.1 Classes of verbs

    verb roots can be divided into those yielding an intransitive stem, and those yielding a transitive stem. The only inflectional criterion distinguishing the two classes is the third person prefix ta- , which can only occur on transitive stems. Thus, transitive yawanka ‘kill’ can take ta- , but intransitive yaruwa ‘laugh’ does not .

      1. convfemgrme-217
        iyawë i-yawë 3-loc
        chipëkë chipëkë because
        tayawankase ta-yawanka-se 3p-kill-pst
        ‘therefore, he killed her (auto)’ (Conversación sobre malos augurios y otros: 217)
      2. convrisamaj-42
        yaruwa=kontomo yaruwa-∅=-kontomo laugh-ipfv=pl
        yatum yatum day
        ponoko   ***
        ‘They laugh every day.’ (Conversación sobre la risa entre GrMe y ElPe: 42)
